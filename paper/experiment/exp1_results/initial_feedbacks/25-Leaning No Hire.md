## Question

Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you handle the situation?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer, I encountered a significant disagreement with a colleague, Jake, regarding the database design for a critical feature in a large-scale web application. Jake favored a NoSQL database for its flexibility, while I strongly believed that a relational SQL database was essential for ensuring data integrity and consistency, which were crucial for the project's long-term success. Acknowledging the weight of this decision, I took full ownership of the resolution process and implemented a structured approach to reconcile our differing perspectives.

I initiated a focused meeting with Jake, where we both presented our arguments in detail. I actively listened to his insights, which allowed me to appreciate the potential advantages of NoSQL, while I clearly articulated my concerns regarding data integrity and its implications for the project's overall viability. To facilitate an objective evaluation, I proposed developing two prototypes‚Äîone using NoSQL and the other using SQL‚Äîenabling us to assess their performance, scalability, and maintainability in a real-world context.

I led the development of the SQL prototype, taking responsibility for defining the database schema, optimizing query performance, and establishing critical performance metrics, including query response times, data retrieval accuracy, and system load under simulated user conditions. I collaborated closely with our team, ensuring that their contributions were integrated into both the design and testing phases. Jake led the NoSQL prototype's schema design, and I supported him in optimizing its query capabilities. To ensure unbiased results, I implemented a rigorous double-blind testing methodology, meticulously tracking all metrics throughout the evaluation process. Our analysis revealed that the SQL prototype outperformed the NoSQL option, achieving a remarkable 30% improvement in data retrieval times and a 15% reduction in error rates.

After compiling our findings, I organized a comprehensive follow-up meeting with the broader team to present our results and foster an inclusive discussion. I facilitated this conversation by encouraging team members to share their insights and feedback, ensuring everyone felt valued and engaged. This not only reinforced our decision to adopt the SQL approach but also strengthened our team dynamic and led to a more cohesive implementation strategy.

This experience underscored the importance of open communication and evidence-based decision-making in my leadership style. I learned that effective leadership involves not just driving decisions but also fostering collaboration and inclusivity. In subsequent projects, I have proactively sought feedback from stakeholders earlier in the process, ensuring that diverse perspectives are integrated into decision-making. By embracing this mindset, I aim to influence broader teams and projects, ensuring our decisions are technically sound and strategically aligned with the company's vision, thereby maximizing our collective impact.
