## Question

Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you handle the situation?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer, I faced a significant disagreement with a colleague, Jake, regarding the database design for a critical feature in a large-scale web application. Jake advocated for a NoSQL database due to its flexibility, while I firmly believed that a relational SQL database was essential for maintaining data integrity and consistency, which were crucial for our long-term project goals. Recognizing the weight of this decision, I took full ownership and initiated a structured resolution process.

To address our differing viewpoints, I organized a focused meeting with Jake, ensuring we both had ample opportunity to present our perspectives. I actively listened to his rationale, which helped me understand the potential advantages of NoSQL. In turn, I clearly articulated my concerns regarding data integrity and its direct implications for our project's success. To facilitate an objective evaluation, I proposed that we develop two prototypes‚Äîone using NoSQL and the other with SQL‚Äîallowing us to assess their performance, scalability, and maintainability in a real-world context.

I independently built the SQL prototype, writing approximately 500 lines of code, including the schema, queries, and a simple API. I also created test scripts that simulated our expected load and ran performance tests on both prototypes. My analysis revealed that the SQL prototype averaged a query response time of 45ms for complex queries, compared to NoSQL's 120ms. Additionally, SQL maintained 100% data consistency under load, while NoSQL had a consistency rate of 98.5%. The development time for SQL was 2 days, while NoSQL took 3 days due to the learning curve. I compiled these findings into a detailed comparison document, which included visual representations of the data, making it easier for the team to understand the trade-offs.

After compiling our findings, I organized a comprehensive follow-up meeting with the broader team to present our results and encourage an inclusive discussion. I shared my document outlining my perspective with supporting evidence before the meeting, which helped frame the conversation. During the discussion, I restated my key points and actively sought feedback, ensuring that everyone felt valued and engaged. I acknowledged Jake's valid points, which fostered a respectful dialogue and reinforced our decision to adopt the SQL approach. This collaborative effort not only solidified our technical decision but also strengthened our team dynamic and implementation strategy.

This experience underscored the importance of open communication and evidence-based decision-making. I learned that effective leadership involves not only driving decisions but also nurturing collaboration and inclusivity. Moving forward, I will continue to prioritize clarity in my contributions while actively seeking input from all team members in decision-making processes. I plan to document my contributions more clearly and create decision logs to track who proposed what and why decisions were made. This proactive approach will enhance team morale and drive superior outcomes aligned with our project goals, ultimately contributing to the success of our initiatives. By embracing this mindset, I aim to influence broader teams and projects, ensuring our decisions are technically sound and strategically aligned with the company's vision.
