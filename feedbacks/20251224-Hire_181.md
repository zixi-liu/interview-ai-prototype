## Question

Assume you are given a task to design a system. How would you do it? How would you resolve ambiguity?

## Answer

When tasked with designing a system, I adopt a proactive and structured approach that emphasizes ownership, strategic problem-solving, and measurable outcomes. I initiate the process by conducting comprehensive stakeholder interviews and workshops to gather diverse insights and ensure alignment on project objectives. For instance, in a recent project aimed at overhauling our customer onboarding process, I facilitated a series of workshops that identified 30 critical requirements. This rigorous engagement not only addressed user pain points but also aligned with our strategic goals, resulting in a 20% increase in customer satisfaction scores, as measured by post-onboarding surveys.

After gathering requirements, I led a thorough feasibility study where I evaluated various technology stacks and compliance considerations. I determined that adopting a microservices architecture was the optimal choice, projecting a 40% reduction in deployment times and significantly enhancing system resilience‚Äîcrucial given our user base growth of 150% over the past year. This decision was informed by data from previous projects, where similar architectures demonstrated substantial performance improvements, thus minimizing risk. Specifically, this architecture allowed us to scale different components independently, enabling us to handle 5x more traffic without major infrastructure changes, while improving system response time by 40% and reducing merge conflicts by 60%.

I then developed a comprehensive design proposal that included detailed system architecture diagrams, data flow charts, and user interface wireframes. I organized stakeholder review meetings where I presented our design, actively solicited feedback, and addressed ambiguities through structured discussions. For example, when a stakeholder expressed concerns about the complexity of my initial design, I facilitated a detailed discussion about the trade-offs, ultimately simplifying certain aspects while maintaining scalability for critical components. This collaborative approach not only refined our design but also fostered a sense of ownership among team members, leading to a 25% improvement in user engagement metrics.

Taking full ownership of the project, I created extensive documentation, including technical specifications and user stories, which served as a clear roadmap for the development team. I utilized collaboration tools like Slack and Confluence to maintain transparent communication, ensuring that all team members were aligned and informed throughout the project lifecycle. My leadership was pivotal in cultivating a culture of collaboration; I encouraged team contributions, recognized individual efforts, and facilitated knowledge sharing, significantly boosting team morale and productivity.

During the project, I faced challenges such as conflicting stakeholder priorities. To navigate these complexities, I created a priority matrix that illustrated the impact versus effort of various features, facilitating discussions among stakeholders to reach consensus on high-impact features. This allowed us to focus on delivering value early in the process. As a result of my strategic approach and decisive ownership, we achieved a 30% reduction in onboarding time. By effectively influencing cross-functional teams and ensuring alignment with organizational goals, I delivered a technically robust product that met user needs. This experience not only underscores my ability to lead impactful initiatives but also highlights my commitment to fostering a collaborative environment, continuous learning, and innovation in system design. I continuously reflect on these experiences to identify areas for personal growth, ensuring that I evolve as a leader and engineer in a fast-paced environment.
