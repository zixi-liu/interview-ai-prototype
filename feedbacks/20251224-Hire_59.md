**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Describe a time when you had to give someone difficult feedback. How did you handle it?

## Answer

In my role as a senior software developer at a tech company, I encountered a pivotal moment when I needed to provide constructive feedback to a junior developer, Alex, who, despite his strong coding abilities, was struggling with documentation‚Äîa critical element for our team's efficiency and scalability. Recognizing the significant impact of documentation on both individual and team performance, I took full ownership of the mentoring process, aiming to cultivate a culture of accountability and continuous improvement.

I initiated a one-on-one meeting with Alex in a supportive environment, where I first acknowledged his technical strengths to establish rapport and trust. I then clearly articulated the importance of effective documentation, emphasizing its direct influence on our team's productivity and the onboarding process for new hires. To ensure my feedback was actionable, I provided specific examples of well-documented code from our projects and curated tailored resources to enhance his documentation practices.

To create a structured and measurable improvement plan, I set clear objectives targeting a 50% increase in the clarity and completeness of his documentation over the next three months. I implemented regular review sessions to provide timely feedback on his drafts, empowering Alex to take ownership of his development while fostering a culture of shared responsibility within our team. Initially, I conducted a documentation audit that revealed a coverage score of only 15%. This allowed us to track progress effectively.

The results were substantial: by the end of the follow-up period, Alex achieved a remarkable increase in documentation coverage to 75%. Additionally, the number of questions other developers asked about his code decreased from approximately 3-4 questions per week to just 1 per month, and his pull requests were approved faster due to improved clarity. I shared these metrics with Alex in a follow-up conversation, which he found motivating and affirming.

Recognizing the broader implications of this experience, I took the initiative to organize a team workshop focused on best practices for documentation. I presented data that demonstrated the positive impact of clear documentation on our workflow, leading to a team-wide commitment to prioritize clarity and completeness. Post-workshop surveys indicated a 40% increase in team members' confidence in their documentation skills, and our project delivery timelines improved by 20% due to reduced onboarding time for new team members.

This experience not only reinforced my commitment to mentorship but also highlighted the importance of delivering constructive feedback in a manner that promotes growth and accountability. I learned that adapting my mentoring style to suit individual learning preferences is crucial; for instance, I had previously struggled with another developer and realized that real-time code reviews and involving their manager were effective strategies. This equipped me with valuable insights that I continue to apply as I mentor other team members, ensuring our collective output remains high-quality and maintainable. Through this process, I not only enhanced Alex's skills but also significantly contributed to our team's culture of collaboration and shared responsibility. My ownership of this mentoring initiative and the measurable improvements across the organization demonstrate my ability to influence and drive enhancements that align with our goals for high performance and continuous learning.
