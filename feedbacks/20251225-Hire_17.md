# Red Flag Evaluation (20251225)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Have you ever been in a situation where another team and yours were creating a similar product? What happened?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Engineer at a tech company, I encountered a critical situation when I discovered that my team and another team were both independently developing automation tools for customer service. Recognizing the potential for resource duplication and the risk of delivering suboptimal solutions to our customers, I took full ownership of the situation. I initiated a strategic collaboration to unify our efforts, believing it was essential for maximizing our impact and delivering a superior product.

I led the technical integration by designing the architecture for merging the two codebases and writing the integration layer myself, which consisted of about 1,500 lines of code. I organized and facilitated a kickoff meeting with the other team to align our project scopes and objectives. During this meeting, I conducted a detailed analysis of both tools, identifying key features, strengths, and areas for improvement. My findings indicated that by integrating the most impactful elements from both projects, we could create a tool that not only met but exceeded our initial goals. I developed a comprehensive integration plan and timeline, breaking it down into phases, and I coordinated the code review process to ensure both teams' code met quality standards.

To drive execution, I established a structured project timeline with specific milestones and deliverables, assigning clear accountability measures for both teams. I implemented a robust feedback loop with daily stand-ups and bi-weekly check-ins, allowing us to adapt quickly to challenges. When we encountered conflicting priorities and differing communication styles, I proactively facilitated weekly alignment sessions and created a shared dashboard to monitor our progress and key performance indicators. I also created objective criteria for feature evaluation, which helped resolve conflicts about which features to keep and which to drop. This data-driven approach ensured that decisions were made based on user value, technical quality, and maintainability, fostering a culture of constructive conflict resolution.

Understanding the importance of team engagement, I prioritized celebrating key milestones and encouraging open dialogue, which significantly boosted morale and collaboration. To address concerns about ownership, I emphasized that we were creating something better together and made sure to highlight contributions from both teams in our updates. As a direct result of our unified efforts, we successfully delivered a feature-rich tool that increased customer service efficiency by 30%, reduced response times by 40%, and elevated user satisfaction scores from 3.5 to 4.6 out of 5, as confirmed by customer feedback surveys. Additionally, within two weeks of launch, 95% of agents were using the integrated tool, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to either tool developed separately.

Reflecting on this experience, I learned the critical importance of taking ownership and facilitating cross-team collaboration to achieve successful outcomes. I recognized that my ability to align priorities, foster open communication, and create structured processes for handling disagreements was vital in overcoming challenges. Moving forward, I am committed to applying these lessons in future initiatives, ensuring that I continue to deliver high-quality solutions that align with organizational goals and drive measurable impact. I will focus on involving all team members earlier in the planning process and creating better communication channels from the start to enhance collaboration and transparency.
