## Question

Describe a time when you had to work simultaneously on both high-priority urgent projects as well as long-term projects. How did you go about handling both?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer, I faced a critical situation where I had to manage a high-priority client issue that threatened the stability of a live product while simultaneously leading a long-term strategic software development initiative. I took complete ownership of both projects by implementing a prioritization framework that evaluated client impact, revenue implications, and strategic alignment. This structured approach allowed me to resolve the client issue in just three days, which was faster than our service level agreement, restoring client trust and preventing approximately $25,000 in potential lost revenue. The client expressed their appreciation in a thank-you email, stating our response was 'exceptional' and that we 'saved them from a major disruption,' which contributed to their decision to renew their contract with us for another year.

To effectively manage these competing priorities, I created a detailed action plan that included a Gantt chart for the long-term project, clearly outlining timelines and key milestones, alongside a Kanban board to track the urgent issue's progress. I organized and led prioritization meetings with my team, where I presented insights from our framework, fostering a collaborative environment that ensured alignment with our strategic objectives. My communication style encouraged team members to share their insights and concerns, which allowed us to remain agile and responsive to any changes in project dynamics.

Recognizing the unique strengths of my team members, I strategically delegated tasks based on their capabilities. For instance, I assigned a junior developer with experience in database migrations to handle that aspect of the long-term project, ensuring they were equipped through a pair programming session and providing thorough documentation. I also delegated the API documentation task to another team member who excelled in writing, checking in daily to offer support. This approach not only empowered my team but also resulted in a significant increase in productivity, with a 30% improvement as we streamlined our processes.

To maintain momentum on the urgent client issue, I instituted daily stand-up meetings that I facilitated, promoting open communication and swiftly addressing any blockers. I dedicated specific time blocks each day‚Äîmornings for urgent work and afternoons for long-term projects‚Äîusing the Pomodoro technique to enhance focus. This structure helped mitigate the mental strain of context switching between urgent bug fixing and long-term architecture work.

As a direct outcome of my structured and hands-on approach, we not only resolved the client issue promptly but also kept the long-term project on schedule, achieving key milestones, including a successful beta launch that garnered positive feedback from stakeholders. This was reflected in a 35% increase in user engagement metrics post-launch and a projected 20% increase in revenue from the new features.

Throughout this experience, I faced challenges in balancing the urgency of the client issue with the strategic vision of the long-term project. I overcame these challenges by continuously reassessing priorities using a priority matrix and maintaining open lines of communication with my team and stakeholders, ensuring alignment and transparency. I learned the importance of being systematic and transparent about priorities, which reinforced my understanding of effective prioritization and agile responses. Moving forward, I am committed to applying these lessons to drive both immediate and long-term success in future projects, leveraging my ability to balance urgent needs with strategic goals effectively.
