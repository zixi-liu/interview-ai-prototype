## Question

Tell me about a time when you had to deal with a significant change at work. How did you adapt to this change?

## Answer

In my previous role as a senior software developer at a large tech company, I took complete ownership of a critical transition from a monolithic architecture to a microservices architecture, which was essential for enhancing our system's scalability and performance. I developed a comprehensive vision and roadmap that aligned with our company's long-term objectives while addressing immediate business needs.

To prepare for this transition, I proactively enrolled in an advanced online course on microservices architecture, dedicating my personal time to deepen my expertise. I applied this knowledge directly by architecting and developing several key microservices, which not only reinforced my learning but also delivered immediate value to our team. I created a reusable service template that reduced the time to create a new microservice from about two weeks to just three days. I also documented best practices, which significantly decreased support questions from 20-25 per week to about 5-8 per week, as evidenced by tracking the usage of the template, which was utilized 15 times in the first three months.

Recognizing a gap in our team's knowledge-sharing practices, I initiated structured study groups and organized hands-on workshops where we built small microservices applications together. My presentations on service communication patterns and distributed system challenges helped clarify complex concepts for my teammates, leading to improved understanding and engagement. Feedback from team members indicated that my explanations made a significant difference in their grasp of the material.

Throughout the transition, I maintained open and transparent communication with both my team and management. I provided bi-weekly updates that included detailed metrics on our progress, showcasing a 50% increase in deployment frequency and a 40% reduction in system downtime, tracked by our CI/CD pipeline and monitoring tools. I also created monitoring dashboards that reduced our mean time to detect problems from about two hours to just 15 minutes, which was crucial for our operational efficiency.

My leadership was particularly evident when I led the development of a critical microservice for one of our flagship projects. I facilitated decision-making processes, coordinated with cross-functional teams, and ensured that our design adhered to best practices while meeting business requirements. One of the most significant challenges we faced was integrating legacy systems with the new microservices architecture. I took the initiative to map out dependencies and developed a phased migration strategy that minimized disruptions. Personally, I tackled the most complex integration issues, collaborating closely with the legacy systems team to ensure a seamless transition. For instance, I implemented a dual-running system during the migration phase, which allowed us to gradually shift traffic to the new microservices without impacting user experience.

The impact of our transition was substantial. Within three months, we achieved a 50% increase in deployment frequency and a 40% reduction in system downtime. These improvements directly enhanced our application's scalability and performance. My contributions, particularly in developing key features that improved system reliability and efficiency, were instrumental in achieving these outcomes. This experience not only honed my technical skills but also reinforced my ability to lead and adapt in the face of significant change. I learned the importance of resilience and adaptability, and I take pride in having played a key role in driving our team‚Äôs transformation during this critical period, ultimately aligning our efforts with the broader objectives of the organization while fostering a culture of collaboration and continuous learning.
