## Question

Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you handle the situation?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer, I faced a significant disagreement with a colleague, Jake, regarding the database design for a critical feature in a large-scale web application. Jake advocated for a NoSQL database due to its flexibility, while I believed that a relational SQL database was essential for maintaining data integrity and consistency, crucial for the project's long-term success. Recognizing the importance of this decision, I took complete ownership of the resolution process and developed a strategic plan to bridge our differing perspectives.

I organized a focused meeting with Jake, where we both presented our arguments in detail. I actively listened to his insights, which helped me appreciate the potential advantages of NoSQL. At the same time, I articulated my concerns regarding data integrity and its implications for our project's success. To ensure an objective evaluation, I proposed that we develop two prototypes‚Äîone utilizing NoSQL and the other with SQL‚Äîallowing us to assess their performance, scalability, and maintainability in a real-world context.

I personally built the SQL prototype, writing about 500 lines of code, including the schema, queries, and a simple API. I also created test scripts that simulated our expected load and ran performance tests on both prototypes. My analysis focused on several key metrics: query response time (SQL averaged 45ms for complex queries compared to NoSQL's 120ms), data consistency (SQL had zero inconsistencies while NoSQL had some), and development time (SQL took 2 days, whereas NoSQL took 3 days due to the learning curve). I compiled these findings into a comparison document, which I presented to the team using charts and specific numbers to clarify the decision-making process.

Throughout the development, I ensured that my contributions were distinguishable by preparing my arguments in advance with specific examples and data. I actively participated in team discussions, sharing a document outlining my perspective with supporting evidence before the meeting. During discussions, I reiterated my key points and invited feedback on my reasoning while acknowledging Jake's valid points, which fostered a respectful dialogue and ensured my perspective was considered seriously.

After compiling our findings, I organized a comprehensive follow-up meeting with the broader team to present our results and encourage an inclusive discussion. I facilitated this conversation by inviting team members to share their insights and feedback, ensuring everyone felt valued and engaged. This approach not only reinforced our decision to adopt the SQL approach but also strengthened our team dynamic and led to a more cohesive implementation strategy.

This experience underscored the importance of open communication and evidence-based decision-making. I learned that effective leadership involves not only driving decisions but also fostering collaboration and inclusivity. Reflecting on this experience, I recognize that I could have engaged additional stakeholders earlier in the process to gather more diverse feedback. Moving forward, I will prioritize clarity in my contributions and proactively seek input from all team members in decision-making processes. This approach enhances team morale and drives superior outcomes aligned with our project goals, ultimately contributing to the success of our initiatives. By embracing this mindset, I aim to influence broader teams and projects, ensuring our decisions are technically sound and strategically aligned with the company's vision, thereby maximizing our collective impact.
