**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me about a time when you had to prioritize your tasks quickly.

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer at a high-growth tech startup, I encountered a critical situation when a significant bug began causing our top client's system to crash every 2-3 hours, impacting around 200 daily users and posing a potential revenue loss of up to $100,000. Recognizing the urgency, I took complete ownership of the situation and led the response effort. I promptly communicated the severity of the bug to my team through a message in our Slack channel, emphasizing the need to prioritize fixing the bug over ongoing feature development. I also reached out directly to my team lead for their support, which they provided by reassigning one of my feature tasks to another developer, allowing me to focus on the bug.

I convened an emergency meeting where I facilitated a structured discussion to establish clear objectives and assigned specific responsibilities to each team member, ensuring alignment and accountability. I conducted a thorough root cause analysis within two hours, identifying the core issue behind the disruption. I then coordinated the implementation of a fix, overseeing the deployment of a patch that restored full functionality for the client within the same day, successfully reducing their downtime from an estimated 12 hours to just 30 minutes. This decisive action not only exceeded the client‚Äôs expectations but also resulted in a 15% increase in their satisfaction score in our subsequent feedback survey. The client later expressed their gratitude in a thank-you email, stating that the fix saved them from potential data loss that could have cost them around $10,000, and our account manager informed me that this quick response was a key factor in the client renewing their annual contract with us.

Post-crisis, I recognized the importance of maintaining development momentum while ensuring quality. I developed a structured plan that broke down the remaining tasks into smaller, manageable components, set clear deadlines, and assigned specific responsibilities to each team member. This approach fostered accountability and collaboration within the team. I actively monitored progress, provided support where necessary, and ensured we met our original release schedule without compromising quality. This collaborative effort culminated in the timely delivery of the feature, and the client expressed their appreciation for our responsiveness and dedication.

To prevent similar issues in the future, I initiated the implementation of enhanced testing protocols, which included automated regression tests and comprehensive code reviews. I tracked the effectiveness of these changes by monitoring bug recurrence rates and team efficiency metrics, which demonstrated a 40% reduction in critical issues over the following months. Additionally, I held regular feedback sessions with my team to gather insights on our processes, leading to actionable improvements that further strengthened our development practices. Reflecting on my leadership style, I realized I could have delegated some of the feature work earlier and communicated the timeline impact more clearly. Moving forward, I plan to leverage these insights to enhance our team's efficiency and strengthen client relationships in future projects, ensuring that I continue to drive measurable impact and foster a culture of excellence.
