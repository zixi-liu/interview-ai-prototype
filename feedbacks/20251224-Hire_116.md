# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Have you ever been in a situation where another team and yours were creating a similar product? What happened?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Engineer at a tech company, I encountered a critical situation where my team and another team were both developing similar automation tools for customer service. Understanding the risk of resource duplication and misalignment, I took full ownership of the situation by leading a strategic cross-team collaboration to unify our efforts and deliver a superior product.

I personally led the technical integration, designing the architecture for merging the two codebases and writing the integration layer myself, which comprised about 1,500 lines of code. I organized a kickoff meeting with the other team to clearly define our project scopes and objectives. I conducted a thorough analysis of each tool's unique strengths and weaknesses, which allowed me to propose a detailed integration strategy that leveraged the best features from both projects. I created the integration plan and timeline, breaking it down into phases, and coordinated the code review process to ensure both teams' code met quality standards.

To ensure effective execution, I developed a comprehensive project timeline with specific milestones and established a structured feedback loop that included daily stand-ups and bi-weekly check-ins. I also created dashboards to track key performance metrics, such as customer service ticket resolution time, which decreased by 35%, agent productivity, which increased by 28%, and user satisfaction scores, which improved from 3.5 to 4.6 out of 5. Additionally, within two weeks of launch, 95% of agents were using the integrated tool, demonstrating its effectiveness compared to either tool in isolation.

When we faced challenges, such as conflicts over which features to keep, I addressed this by creating objective criteria for feature evaluation based on user value, technical quality, and maintainability. I facilitated discussions where both teams could present their cases, ensuring decisions were made based on data rather than emotions. I emphasized that we were creating something better together, not replacing anyone's work, and I acknowledged contributions from both teams in our updates.

To maintain team morale, I organized team-building activities and fostered an environment of open dialogue, which significantly boosted engagement across both teams. I celebrated key milestones throughout the project, not just at the end, to reinforce our progress and maintain enthusiasm. This project not only streamlined our processes but also established a culture of collaboration and innovation, setting a strong precedent for future initiatives.

This experience reinforced my belief in the power of cross-team collaboration and the importance of taking ownership to drive projects to successful outcomes. I am committed to applying these lessons in future initiatives, ensuring that I continue to deliver high-quality solutions that align with organizational goals and drive measurable impact. Additionally, I learned the critical value of involving all team members early in the planning process and creating structured communication channels, which I will prioritize in all collaborative efforts moving forward.
