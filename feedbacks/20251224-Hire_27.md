# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me a time when you had a different opinion than the rest of the team. How did you handle it?

## Answer

Situation: In my previous role as a software developer at a SaaS company, our team was tasked with improving the performance of our main product. The majority of the team favored a complete rewrite of a significant portion of the legacy code, believing this was the only way to address the performance issues. Task: While I understood their reasoning, I analyzed the codebase and concluded that a complete rewrite was risky and unnecessary. Instead, I proposed targeted optimization and refactoring of specific inefficient code segments, which I believed would be more efficient and less resource-intensive. Action: Initially, the team was skeptical and thought I was being too conservative, especially since some members were excited about using new technologies in a rewrite. I acknowledged their concerns and demonstrated respect for their opinions while firmly presenting data that highlighted the risks and costs associated with a complete rewrite. I prepared a detailed presentation that included a performance analysis of the current system, identified bottlenecks, and showcased how targeted refactoring could resolve these issues. To further substantiate my proposal, I took the initiative to refactor a small portion of the code as a proof of concept. In this proof of concept, I measured response time, which improved by 45%, memory usage, which reduced by 30%, and code complexity, which decreased by 25%. I ensured the relevance of these metrics by using the same load testing scenarios that we employed in production. The time to implement my changes was just 2 weeks compared to the 3 months estimated for a complete rewrite. Result: After reviewing my analysis and the results of the proof of concept, the team gradually came around to my approach. We successfully improved the system's performance without the high costs and risks of a complete rewrite. The project was completed ahead of schedule and under budget, with the improved performance metrics well-received by stakeholders. This experience taught me the importance of involving the team in the analysis process, being respectful and collaborative, and backing up opinions with data. I learned that persuasion works better when you engage others in the process, and I will apply these lessons in future scenarios where dissenting opinions arise.

Result: After reviewing my analysis and the results of the proof of concept, the team agreed to try the refactoring approach. We were able to significantly improve the system's performance without the high costs and risks of a complete rewrite. The project was completed ahead of schedule and under budget, and the improved performance metrics were well-received by stakeholders. This experience taught me the value of backing up opinions with data and analysis, the importance of effective communication in team settings, and how taking initiative can lead to better decision-making.
