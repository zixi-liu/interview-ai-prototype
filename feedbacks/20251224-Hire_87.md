**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you handle the situation?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer, I faced a significant disagreement with a colleague, Jake, regarding the database design for a critical feature in a large-scale web application. Jake advocated for a NoSQL database due to its flexibility, while I strongly believed that a relational SQL database was essential for maintaining data integrity and consistency, especially given the high stakes involved in this decision. Recognizing the importance of this choice, I took full ownership of the situation and initiated a structured resolution process.

I organized a meeting where both Jake and I could present our arguments in detail. I actively listened to his perspective, ensuring I fully understood his rationale, while I clearly articulated my concerns about data integrity and its alignment with our long-term project goals. To facilitate an informed decision, I proposed that we develop two prototypes‚Äîone using NoSQL and the other using SQL. This approach would allow us to evaluate their performance, scalability, and maintainability in a real-world context.

I built the SQL prototype myself, writing about 500 lines of code, including the schema, queries, and a simple API. I also created test scripts that simulated our expected load and ran performance tests on both prototypes. My analysis revealed that the SQL prototype averaged a query response time of 45ms compared to NoSQL's 120ms for complex queries, and it maintained 100% data consistency under load, while NoSQL had a 98.5% consistency rate. Additionally, SQL took only 2 days to develop, whereas NoSQL took 3 days due to the learning curve. I compiled these findings into a comparison document with visual charts that clearly highlighted the advantages of the SQL approach.

After rigorous analysis, I organized a follow-up meeting with the broader team to present our results and foster a collaborative discussion. I encouraged team members to share their insights and made sure to acknowledge Jake's valid points, which helped create an inclusive atmosphere. This collaborative effort not only reinforced our decision to adopt the SQL approach but also cultivated a sense of ownership and engagement within the team, leading to a stronger team dynamic and a more cohesive implementation strategy.

This experience reinforced my belief in the power of open communication, evidence-based decision-making, and the importance of empowering team members throughout the process. I learned that effective leadership involves not only driving decisions but also facilitating collaboration and fostering a culture of inclusivity. Moving forward, I will continue to prioritize clarity in my contributions, ensuring that all team members feel valued and heard in decision-making processes. I also plan to document my contributions more clearly from the start and proactively communicate my role in project outcomes, as I believe this will enhance team morale and drive superior results aligned with our project goals, ultimately contributing to the success of our initiatives.
