## Question

How would you design/test a product to make sure its diverse/inclusive to all users?

## Answer

In my previous role as the lead engineer for a health and fitness app, I took full ownership of a comprehensive redesign initiative aimed at enhancing inclusivity and accessibility for our diverse user base. Recognizing the critical importance of our app resonating with various demographics, I initiated a robust user research program that included over 200 surveys, 15 in-depth interviews, and three focus groups specifically targeting individuals with disabilities and non-native English speakers. This extensive research allowed me to identify key pain points, such as navigation difficulties and the absence of culturally relevant content, which I prioritized for immediate resolution.

To address these challenges, I led my team in embedding inclusive design principles throughout the development lifecycle. I personally drove the implementation of key features, including adjustable text sizes, high-contrast color schemes, and culturally sensitive content, ensuring compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). I wrote approximately 800 lines of code for the adjustable text size and high-contrast mode features, and I created the accessibility testing scripts, running them myself using screen readers and other assistive technologies. I made strategic trade-offs by prioritizing features that maximized user experience while aligning with our technical capabilities and development timelines, thereby supporting the company‚Äôs broader business objectives.

To validate our design choices, I established and led a diverse beta testing group of 30 individuals, conducting bi-weekly feedback sessions that I facilitated. I actively engaged with participants, fostering an environment of open dialogue to gather their insights. This iterative feedback process was crucial; I meticulously analyzed their responses, which directly informed a significant redesign of our navigation structure. As a result of my leadership, we achieved a 50% reduction in user-reported navigation issues and a 40% increase in overall usability ratings across all demographics. Notably, we saw a 35% increase in users from underrepresented groups, and our app store rating improved from 3.4 to 4.6 stars, with many reviews specifically mentioning the accessibility features.

The impact of my leadership in this initiative was substantial. We recorded a 40% increase in user satisfaction ratings and a 30% boost in downloads within the first month of launch, significantly broadening our market reach and enhancing our brand image. Throughout the project, I faced challenges in balancing feature requests with technical constraints. For instance, I had to navigate conflicting priorities between accessibility features and performance optimizations. I addressed concerns from the product team about the timeline impact of inclusive design by demonstrating data from other companies that showed how inclusive design improved overall product quality. By creating a phased approach where we implemented high-impact, low-effort changes first, I was able to show quick wins that built buy-in for the initiative.

This experience reinforced my belief in the importance of empathy in design and the value of diverse perspectives in creating products that serve all users. I am committed to fostering an inclusive culture in every team I join, leveraging my proactive leadership and collaboration skills to drive measurable outcomes. My focus on clear ownership and accountability, coupled with a data-driven approach to decision-making, ensures that I contribute effectively to both team success and organizational goals. I have learned that true leadership involves not only guiding a team but also embracing feedback and continuously evolving to meet the needs of all users, which I will carry forward into future projects.
