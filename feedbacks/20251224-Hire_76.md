## Question

Describe a time you received tough or critical feedback

## Answer

In my role as a Senior Software Developer at a tech company, I led the development of a critical component aimed at enhancing our application‚Äôs scalability for a major client project. During a project review, my manager provided constructive feedback regarding performance inefficiencies in my code. Recognizing the importance of this feedback, I took immediate ownership by scheduling a one-on-one meeting with my manager to gain a deeper understanding of the specific issues and expectations.

During our discussion, I actively sought clarification and engaged in a collaborative dialogue, which allowed me to identify the root causes of the inefficiencies. I conducted a thorough analysis of my codebase, focusing on algorithm efficiency and code complexity. Acknowledging the complexity of some performance issues that resided in legacy code, I dedicated time to understand the existing architecture and collaborated with a senior colleague who specialized in performance optimization. Together, we implemented targeted optimizations that resulted in a remarkable 77% improvement in API response time, reducing it from 1.2 seconds to 280 milliseconds, a 45% decrease in memory usage, and a drop in CPU utilization from 85% to 40% under normal load.

To validate these enhancements, I executed rigorous performance testing and documented all metrics in a performance report, which I shared with my manager. This effort led to a significant 15% increase in client satisfaction scores, as the client reported a 60% improvement in page load times for features utilizing my component. I presented these results in a team meeting, clearly articulating my contributions while emphasizing the collaborative efforts that led to our success. I also encouraged my colleagues to adopt similar optimization strategies, fostering a culture of continuous improvement within the team.

To ensure the sustainability of our optimizations, I documented my work in our project management tool, detailing the before-and-after metrics. I presented these improvements during our team retrospective meeting and included this work in my performance review with my manager's support. Additionally, I authored a technical blog post about the optimization techniques I used, which was published on our company's engineering blog, further enhancing recognition for our team's efforts.

Throughout this process, I faced challenges, such as refactoring code that other team members were actively using. To mitigate disruptions, I created feature branches and sought early feedback from my peers. I also had to learn new profiling tools, which I accomplished through online tutorials and support from our DevOps team. This experience taught me the importance of proactive communication and early feedback, reinforcing my belief that feedback is a valuable tool for growth, even when it is difficult to hear.

Now, I make performance optimization a regular part of my development process. I approach every piece of code with performance implications in mind, conduct code reviews with a focus on identifying potential bottlenecks, and maintain a checklist of common performance issues. My commitment to continuous learning and improvement has positioned me as a key contributor to our ongoing success, and I am eager to bring this mindset to future challenges.
