## Question

Tell me about a time when you had a hard time working with someone in your team. How did you handle it?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software engineer, I led a cross-functional team tasked with developing a new mobile application under a tight deadline. Early in the project, I recognized that one of my team members, John, had a working style that often led to misalignment with our project objectives, which posed a risk to our timeline. Understanding the critical nature of our goals, I took full ownership of the situation and initiated a series of strategic actions to realign our efforts.

I arranged a one-on-one meeting with John to gain insight into his perspective and working habits. During this discussion, I clearly articulated our shared objectives and the importance of alignment for our success. I recognized that my communication style needed to adapt to better support John‚Äôs approach, as he preferred written communication over verbal discussions. I started sending detailed written updates and questions instead of relying solely on verbal communication. This adjustment not only made our interactions more effective but also allowed John time to process information before responding.

To foster collaboration, I proposed structured weekly check-ins that aligned with his preferred working style, ensuring we remained focused on our goals. Initially, John was resistant to more frequent check-ins, viewing them as disruptive. I addressed this by making the check-ins shorter and more focused, scheduling them at times that worked for him. I also organized targeted team-building activities, such as pair programming sessions, which were more work-related and helped improve trust and communication among all team members.

As a result of these initiatives, I tracked several key metrics that demonstrated our progress: team satisfaction scores improved from 3.1 to 4.4 out of 5 over two months, the number of miscommunications decreased from about 3-4 per week to maybe 1 per week, and code review turnaround time improved significantly as John became more responsive. Additionally, our project velocity increased from completing 8 story points per sprint to 12 story points per sprint. This improvement enabled us to complete the project two weeks ahead of schedule, which directly contributed to a 15% increase in client satisfaction ratings, as reflected in their feedback praising both the quality of the application and the enhanced teamwork throughout the development process.

This experience reinforced my belief in the power of empathy and proactive communication in leadership. By decisively owning the situation and implementing these changes, I ensured that our project not only met but exceeded expectations. I learned the importance of continuous assessment of team dynamics and adaptability, which has shaped me into a more effective leader capable of influencing positive outcomes across teams and significantly contributing to our organizational goals. Moving forward, I plan to set clearer metrics from the start for any team interventions and involve the team in designing solutions to ensure buy-in and effectiveness.
