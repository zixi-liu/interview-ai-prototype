# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Describe a time when there was a conflict within your team. How did you help resolve the conflict? Did you do anything to prevent it in the future?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer at a mid-sized tech company, we were working on a significant update for our main product when a conflict arose between two team members, John and Sarah, regarding the implementation approach for a crucial feature. John proposed an innovative, untested method, while Sarah advocated for a traditional, proven approach. The disagreement escalated, impacting team morale and productivity.

As a senior member of the team, I took ownership of resolving the conflict and restoring team harmony. I first met with John and Sarah individually to understand their perspectives and acknowledge the merits of both approaches. During this process, I recognized the need for a structured discussion to facilitate a collaborative problem-solving environment. I organized a team meeting where both team members could present their arguments, ensuring they had equal time to share their views, which I timed to maintain fairness.

During the meeting, when tensions rose, I reframed the discussion from "which approach is better" to "what criteria should we use to evaluate both approaches?" This shift helped the team focus on objective evaluation rather than subjective opinions. I also suggested prototyping both methods in a controlled environment, which was a new idea that neither John nor Sarah had proposed. This data-driven approach became the key turning point in our decision-making process.

The prototyping exercise revealed that while John's method had innovative potential, it lacked the stability required for our current project. We ultimately decided to proceed with Sarah's approach but agreed to explore John's method in a future project. This resolution was accepted by both parties, and I made sure to acknowledge the strengths of each approach throughout the discussion, which helped de-escalate the tension.

To prevent future conflicts, I proposed regular team-building activities and open-forum meetings. I tracked the impact of these initiatives and found that team satisfaction survey scores improved from 3.2 to 4.5 out of 5 over three months. Additionally, the number of conflicts reported in our retrospectives decreased from 2-3 per month to about 1 every two months, and attendance at open-forum meetings remained high at 85-90% of the team. The quality of code review feedback also improved, becoming more constructive and less defensive.

This experience taught me the importance of proactive communication, structured conflict resolution, and the value of fostering an inclusive environment where all team members feel heard and respected. I learned that intervening earlier could have mitigated the conflict sooner, and I plan to implement clearer ground rules for discussions in the future. Overall, I believe this experience significantly enhanced my leadership and collaboration skills, contributing positively to our team dynamics.
