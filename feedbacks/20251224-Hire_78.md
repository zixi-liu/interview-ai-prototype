# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Have you ever been in a situation where another team and yours were creating a similar product? What happened?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Engineer at a tech company, I faced a significant challenge when I discovered that my team and another team were both developing automation tools for customer service, risking overlapping functionalities and wasted resources. Recognizing the potential impact on our timelines and deliverables, I took decisive ownership of the situation by leading a strategic cross-team collaboration initiative.

I organized and facilitated a joint meeting with the other team to align our project scopes and objectives. During this meeting, I identified the unique strengths of each tool and proposed a comprehensive integration strategy that would leverage the most effective features from both projects. I led the technical integration by designing the architecture for merging the two codebases and personally writing the integration layer, which consisted of about 1,500 lines of code. I also coordinated the code review process to ensure both teams' code met quality standards and handled the database migration to merge the two systems.

To maintain momentum, I created a detailed integration plan and timeline, breaking it down into phases. I established daily stand-ups to track progress in a shared project management tool, ensuring I was available to unblock team members. When we faced challenges such as differing priorities and communication styles, I proactively organized weekly alignment sessions and developed a shared dashboard to monitor our progress and key performance metrics. This approach fostered transparency and accountability, allowing us to navigate conflicts effectively and keep the project on track.

I prioritized team morale by celebrating key milestones and encouraging open dialogue, which significantly enhanced engagement across both teams. I addressed conflicts regarding feature selection by creating objective criteria for evaluation based on user value, technical quality, and maintainability. I facilitated discussions where both teams could present their cases, ensuring decisions were made based on data rather than emotions, and I made sure to acknowledge contributions from both teams.

As a result of our collaboration, we successfully launched a robust, feature-rich tool that improved customer service efficiency by 35%, evidenced by a decrease in ticket resolution time, an increase in agent productivity by 28%, and a rise in user satisfaction scores from 3.5 to 4.6 out of 5. Additionally, 95% of agents adopted the integrated tool within two weeks of launch. This project not only streamlined our processes but also established a culture of collaboration and innovation between the teams, setting a strong precedent for future initiatives.

This experience reinforced my belief in the power of cross-team collaboration and the importance of taking ownership to drive projects to successful outcomes. I am committed to applying these lessons in future initiatives, ensuring that I continue to deliver high-quality solutions that align with organizational goals and drive measurable impact. I learned the critical value of involving all stakeholders early in the planning process and creating structured communication channels, which I will prioritize in all collaborative efforts moving forward.
