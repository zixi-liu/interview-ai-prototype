# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Have you ever been in a situation where another team and yours were creating a similar product? What happened?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Engineer at a tech company, I faced a critical situation where my team and another team were both developing automation tools for customer service, leading to potential resource duplication and misalignment. Recognizing the inefficiencies, I took full ownership of the situation and led a strategic initiative to unify our efforts, aiming to deliver a superior product that would significantly enhance customer service across the organization.

I organized and led a kickoff meeting with the other team, facilitating detailed discussions to align our project scopes and objectives. To maximize our strengths, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of both tools, identifying key features and areas for improvement. I then crafted a robust integration strategy that combined the most impactful elements from both projects, presenting it with clear rationale and anticipated outcomes.

Taking ownership of the technical integration, I designed the architecture for merging the two codebases and wrote the integration layer myself, comprising about 1,500 lines of code. I coordinated the code review process to ensure both teams' code met quality standards. I created a detailed integration plan and timeline, breaking it down into phases, and handled the database migration to merge the two systems. To ensure execution, I set up daily stand-ups, tracked progress in a shared project management tool, and was available to unblock team members.

To drive accountability and transparency, I established a structured feedback loop that included bi-weekly check-ins and iterative testing phases. When we faced challenges such as conflicting priorities and differing communication styles, I organized weekly alignment sessions and created a shared dashboard to monitor our progress and key performance indicators. I also created objective criteria for feature evaluation, facilitating discussions where both teams could present their cases, ensuring decisions were made based on data rather than emotions. This approach fostered a culture of constructive conflict resolution and maintained morale, as I emphasized that we were creating something better together, not replacing anyone's work.

As a result of our unified efforts, we delivered a feature-rich tool that increased customer service efficiency by 30%, decreased ticket resolution time by 35%, boosted agent productivity by 28%, reduced response times to an average of 200ms, and elevated user satisfaction scores from 3.5 to 4.6 out of 5, with 95% of agents adopting the integrated tool within two weeks. This project not only streamlined our processes but also established a lasting culture of collaboration and innovation, setting a strong precedent for future initiatives.

Reflecting on this experience, I learned the invaluable importance of taking ownership and facilitating cross-team collaboration to achieve successful outcomes. I recognized that my ability to align priorities, foster open communication, and create structured processes for handling disagreements was crucial in overcoming challenges. Moving forward, I am committed to applying these lessons in future initiatives, ensuring that I continue to deliver high-quality solutions that align with organizational goals and drive measurable impact, while leveraging the strengths of all team members in collaborative efforts to achieve exceptional results.
