## Question

Assume you are given a task to design a system. How would you do it? How would you resolve ambiguity?

## Answer

When I am tasked with designing a system, I take a comprehensive and strategic approach that emphasizes clear ownership, robust problem-solving, and measurable outcomes. I begin by conducting in-depth stakeholder interviews and workshops to gather insights and ensure alignment on project objectives. For instance, in a recent project to overhaul our customer onboarding process, I led workshops that identified 30 critical requirements, directly addressing user pain points and aligning with our strategic goals, which resulted in a 20% increase in customer satisfaction scores, as confirmed by post-onboarding surveys.

After gathering requirements, I took ownership of a detailed feasibility study where I evaluated various technology stacks and compliance considerations. I designed a microservices architecture that allowed us to scale different components independently, which was crucial given our user base growth of 150% over the past year. This architecture choice enabled us to handle 5x more traffic without major infrastructure changes, improving system response time by 40% and reducing deployment time from 2 hours to just 15 minutes. My decision was based on data from previous projects, where similar architectures delivered substantial performance improvements, thereby minimizing risk.

I then developed a comprehensive design proposal that included system architecture diagrams, data flow charts, and user interface wireframes. I organized stakeholder review meetings where I presented the design, actively solicited feedback, and resolved ambiguities through structured discussions. For example, when a stakeholder expressed concerns about the complexity of my initial design, I facilitated a detailed discussion about the trade-offs, advocating for the long-term benefits of scalability while also simplifying certain components to meet their needs. This collaborative approach not only refined our design but also fostered a sense of ownership among team members, resulting in a 25% improvement in user engagement metrics.

Taking full ownership of the project, I created extensive documentation, including technical specifications and user stories, which served as a clear roadmap for the development team. I utilized collaboration tools like Slack and Confluence to ensure transparent communication, keeping all team members aligned and informed throughout the project lifecycle. My leadership was instrumental in cultivating a culture of collaboration; I encouraged team contributions, recognized individual efforts, and facilitated knowledge sharing, which significantly boosted team morale and productivity.

During the project, I encountered challenges such as conflicting stakeholder priorities. To navigate these complexities, I led a prioritization workshop where I created a priority matrix to rank requirements based on user impact and business value. This structured approach enabled us to focus on high-impact features first, ensuring that we delivered value early in the process. As a direct result of my strategic leadership and decisive ownership, we achieved a 30% reduction in onboarding time. By effectively influencing cross-functional teams and ensuring alignment with organizational goals, I delivered a technically robust product that met user needs.

This experience not only underscores my ability to lead impactful initiatives but also reflects my commitment to fostering a collaborative environment, continuous learning, and innovation in system design. I regularly reflect on these experiences to identify areas for personal growth, ensuring that I evolve as a leader and engineer in a fast-paced environment. My proactive approach and focus on measurable outcomes position me as a strong candidate who can drive significant contributions at a senior level within a FAANG organization.
