**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague. How did you handle the situation?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer, I encountered a significant disagreement with a colleague, Jake, regarding the database design for a key feature in a large-scale web application. Jake advocated for a NoSQL database due to its flexibility, while I firmly believed that a relational SQL database was crucial for maintaining data integrity and consistency, especially given the high stakes of the project. Recognizing the importance of this decision, I took full ownership and initiated a structured approach to resolve our differing viewpoints.

I organized a meeting with Jake to present our arguments comprehensively. I actively listened to his perspective, which helped me appreciate the potential advantages of NoSQL, while I articulated my concerns about data integrity and how it aligned with our long-term project objectives. To facilitate a data-driven decision, I proposed that we develop two prototypes‚Äîone utilizing NoSQL and the other using SQL. This would allow us to assess their performance, scalability, and maintainability in a real-world context.

I took the lead in developing the SQL prototype, writing approximately 500 lines of code, including the schema, queries, and a simple API. I also created test scripts that simulated our expected load and ran performance tests on both prototypes. My analysis revealed that SQL outperformed NoSQL significantly, achieving a 3x faster response time for complex queries (averaging 45ms compared to NoSQL's 120ms), maintaining 100% data consistency under load versus NoSQL's 98.5%, and requiring less development time (2 days for SQL versus 3 days for NoSQL due to the learning curve). I compiled these findings into a comparison document with charts and specific metrics, which I presented to the team.

After our analysis, I organized a follow-up meeting with the broader team to share our results and encourage an inclusive discussion. I facilitated the conversation by inviting team members to provide insights and feedback, ensuring that everyone felt heard and valued. This not only reinforced our decision to adopt the SQL approach but also fostered a sense of ownership and engagement within the team, leading to a stronger team dynamic and a more cohesive implementation strategy.

This experience deepened my understanding of the importance of open communication and evidence-based decision-making. I learned that effective leadership involves not just driving decisions but also nurturing collaboration and inclusivity. Moving forward, I plan to document my contributions more clearly and proactively highlight my role in team discussions to ensure recognition of my efforts. I will also continue to prioritize clarity in my contributions, ensuring that all team members feel valued and included in decision-making processes. This approach not only enhances team morale but also drives superior outcomes aligned with our project goals, ultimately contributing to the success of our initiatives.
