**Rating**: Hire

## Question

What is the biggest technical challenge you have worked on?

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer at a data analytics firm, I took full ownership of developing a large-scale data processing system for real-time analysis of data streams from millions of IoT devices. I personally designed the overall system architecture and wrote the core data processing pipeline, which comprised about 4,000 lines of Scala code. I made critical technology decisions, selecting Apache Kafka for data ingestion and Apache Spark for processing, which were pivotal for our clients' strategic decision-making.

My architecture enabled us to process over 10 million data points per second, achieving a 300% increase in capacity compared to our legacy system. We tracked key metrics, including a throughput of 50TB/hour, exceeding our target of 30TB/hour, and a p95 latency of 1.2 seconds, well below our 3-second target. The system achieved a 99.8% accuracy rate and 99.9% uptime, while client satisfaction improved significantly, with clients reporting a 60% increase in decision-making speed. I created dashboards to monitor these metrics in real-time and presented them to stakeholders monthly, ensuring transparency and alignment with project goals.

To ensure we met our performance benchmarks, I developed and implemented a rigorous testing strategy. I led the creation of a simulated environment that accurately mirrored expected data loads, which resulted in a 30% reduction in latency and enhanced our clients' decision-making capabilities. I also designed the Kafka consumer groups and Spark job configurations, as well as a monitoring and alerting system to proactively address any issues.

Throughout the project, I prioritized cross-team collaboration by actively engaging with front-end and database teams to ensure seamless integration of our module with existing systems. I organized and facilitated regular sync meetings, fostering an environment of open communication that allowed us to adapt our strategies based on real-time feedback and evolving requirements. When conflicts arose, such as disagreements over database schema optimization, I facilitated discussions where both teams could present their cases, leading to a hybrid approach that satisfied both parties. I also shifted my leadership style to be more supportive, organizing training sessions and pair programming to help team members navigate the complexities of distributed systems.

Reflecting on this experience, I learned the importance of starting with a proof-of-concept early to validate assumptions and the necessity of clear documentation to save time later. I also recognized that communication is critical in complex projects, and regular syncs can prevent many issues. I am eager to bring these lessons and my commitment to excellence to your organization, ensuring that I not only meet but exceed expectations in every endeavor.
