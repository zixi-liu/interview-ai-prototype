# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me about a time when you had to prioritize your tasks quickly.

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Developer at a high-growth tech startup, I encountered a critical situation when a significant bug caused the client's system to crash every 2-3 hours, impacting around 200 daily users and threatening a potential revenue loss of up to $100,000. Recognizing the urgency, I took immediate ownership of the issue by sending a detailed message to our team Slack channel to explain the situation and my decision to prioritize the bug fix. I also directly communicated with my team lead, who supported my decision and helped reassign one of my feature tasks to another developer, allowing me to focus entirely on resolving the bug.

I organized an emergency meeting with my team, where I clearly articulated the bug's potential impact, including an estimated 12 hours of downtime. During this meeting, I defined specific objectives and clarified each team member's responsibilities. I personally conducted a thorough root cause analysis, which I completed within two hours, pinpointing the core issue behind the disruption. I then coordinated the implementation of a fix, overseeing the deployment of a patch that restored full functionality for the client within the same day, reducing their downtime to just 30 minutes. This proactive approach not only exceeded the client's expectations but also resulted in a 15% increase in their satisfaction score in our subsequent feedback survey. The client later expressed their gratitude in a thank-you email, stating that the fix saved them from potential data loss that could have cost them around $10,000, and our account manager noted that this quick response was a key factor in the client renewing their annual contract with us.

After resolving the immediate crisis, I recognized the need to maintain our development momentum while ensuring quality. I developed a structured plan that broke down the remaining tasks into smaller, manageable components, set clear deadlines, and assigned specific responsibilities to each team member, fostering accountability and collaboration. I took the initiative to oversee the execution of this plan, working extra hours to ensure we met our original release schedule without compromising quality. I was responsible for writing approximately 1,500 lines of code for the backend API endpoints, including authentication, data validation, and error handling, while also designing the database schema and writing migration scripts. This collaborative effort culminated in the timely delivery of the feature, and the client expressed their appreciation for our responsiveness and dedication.

To prevent similar issues in the future, I implemented enhanced testing protocols and conducted post-mortem analyses to identify areas for improvement. I tracked the effectiveness of these changes by monitoring bug recurrence rates and team efficiency metrics, which showed a 40% reduction in critical issues over the following months. This experience reinforced my ability to prioritize effectively under pressure and underscored the importance of clear communication and collaboration. I reflected on my leadership style, recognizing that I could have delegated some feature work earlier and communicated the timeline impact more clearly. Moving forward, I plan to leverage these insights to enhance our team's efficiency and strengthen client relationships in future projects, ensuring that I continue to drive measurable impact and foster a culture of excellence.
