## Question

Describe a time when you had to work simultaneously on both high-priority urgent projects as well as long-term projects. How did you go about handling both?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer, I faced a significant challenge where I had to manage a high-priority client issue that jeopardized a live product while simultaneously advancing a long-term strategic software development initiative. Taking full ownership of both responsibilities, I developed a comprehensive prioritization framework that evaluated client impact, revenue implications, and strategic alignment. This structured approach enabled me to make informed, data-driven decisions, resulting in the resolution of the urgent client issue within three days, which was faster than our service level agreement (SLA). This swift action not only restored client trust but also led to a 40% increase in their satisfaction ratings, as confirmed by follow-up surveys. The client even sent us a thank-you email stating that our response was 'exceptional' and that we 'saved them from a major disruption,' which contributed to their decision to renew their contract with us for another year.

To effectively manage these competing priorities, I created a detailed action plan that incorporated a Gantt chart for the long-term project, outlining timelines and key milestones, alongside a Kanban board to monitor the urgent issue's progress. I initiated prioritization meetings with the team, where I presented insights from our framework, fostering collaboration and ensuring alignment with our strategic objectives. My proactive communication style encouraged team members to share their insights and concerns, enabling us to remain agile and responsive to any changes.

Recognizing the unique strengths of my team members, I strategically delegated tasks based on their capabilities. For instance, I assigned a junior developer with experience in database migrations to handle that aspect of the long-term project, ensuring they were equipped through pair programming sessions and close reviews of their work. I also delegated the API documentation task to another team member who excelled in writing. I made it a point to check in daily to see if they needed help, which fostered a culture of accountability and support.

To maintain momentum on the urgent client issue, I instituted daily stand-up meetings that I facilitated, promoting open communication and swiftly addressing any blockers. I dedicated specific time blocks for my work‚Äîmornings for urgent tasks and afternoons for long-term development‚Äîusing the Pomodoro technique to stay focused. This approach helped mitigate the mental drain of context switching. I also set boundaries around my availability for urgent issues, responding only during designated times to minimize interruptions to long-term work.

As a direct result of this structured and hands-on approach, we not only resolved the client issue promptly but also kept the long-term project on schedule, achieving key milestones, including a successful beta launch that garnered positive feedback from stakeholders. This was reflected in a 35% increase in user engagement metrics post-launch and a projected 20% increase in revenue from the new features. I measured my contributions by tracking the time to resolution, the number of critical bugs I personally fixed (four out of six), and the impact of our work, which prevented about $25,000 in potential lost revenue for the client.

Throughout this experience, I faced challenges such as balancing the urgency of the client issue with the strategic vision of the long-term project. I overcame these by continuously reassessing priorities and maintaining open lines of communication with my team and stakeholders, ensuring that everyone was aligned and informed. This reinforced my understanding of effective prioritization and agile responses, highlighting the importance of clear communication and adaptive leadership. I learned to be more proactive and engaged in my leadership style, ensuring that my team felt supported and empowered to deliver exceptional results. Moving forward, I am committed to applying these lessons to drive both immediate and long-term success in future projects, leveraging my ability to balance urgent needs with strategic goals effectively.
