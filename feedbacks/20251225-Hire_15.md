## Question

Describe a time when you had to work simultaneously on both high-priority urgent projects as well as long-term projects. How did you go about handling both?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer, I encountered a critical situation where I had to manage a high-priority client issue that threatened a live product while simultaneously advancing a long-term strategic software development initiative that I was leading. Taking complete ownership of both responsibilities, I developed a prioritization framework that assessed client impact, revenue implications, and strategic alignment, enabling me to make data-driven decisions. This approach allowed me to resolve the urgent client issue within three days, which was faster than the service level agreement, restoring client trust and resulting in a 40% increase in their satisfaction ratings, as confirmed by follow-up surveys. The client even sent us a thank-you email stating our response was 'exceptional' and that we 'saved them from a major disruption,' which contributed to their decision to renew their contract with us for another year.

To effectively manage these competing priorities, I created a comprehensive action plan that included a Gantt chart for the long-term project, detailing timelines and key milestones, and a Kanban board to monitor the urgent issue's progress. I took the initiative to lead prioritization meetings with my team, where I presented insights from our framework, fostering collaboration and ensuring alignment with our strategic objectives. My proactive communication style encouraged team members to share their insights and concerns, which enabled us to remain agile and responsive to any changes in project dynamics.

Recognizing the unique strengths of my team members, I strategically delegated tasks based on their capabilities. For instance, I assigned a junior developer with experience in database migrations to handle that aspect of the long-term project. I ensured they were equipped by conducting a pair programming session to demonstrate the approach and reviewing their work closely. I also delegated the API documentation task to another team member who excelled in writing, checking in daily to see if they needed help. This approach not only empowered my team but also allowed me to focus on strategic decision-making and stakeholder communication.

To maintain momentum on the urgent client issue, I instituted daily stand-up meetings that I facilitated, promoting open communication and swiftly addressing any blockers. I dedicated specific time blocks each day‚Äîmornings for urgent work and afternoons for long-term projects‚Äîusing the Pomodoro technique to stay focused. This structured time management helped me drive critical development tasks and collaborate closely with stakeholders to gather feedback and iterate on our approach, resulting in a 30% increase in productivity.

As a direct outcome of this structured and hands-on approach, we not only resolved the client issue promptly but also kept the long-term project on schedule, achieving key milestones, including a successful beta launch that garnered positive feedback from stakeholders. This was reflected in a 35% increase in user engagement metrics post-launch and a projected 20% increase in revenue from the new features. I also tracked the impact of my contributions, measuring the time to resolution, the number of critical bugs I personally fixed (four out of six), and the potential revenue saved for the client, which amounted to approximately $25,000.

Throughout this experience, I faced challenges in balancing the urgency of the client issue with the strategic vision of the long-term project. I overcame these challenges by continuously reassessing priorities, maintaining open lines of communication with my team and stakeholders, and setting boundaries to manage context switching effectively. This reinforced my understanding of effective prioritization and agile responses, highlighting the importance of clear communication and adaptive leadership. I learned to be more proactive and engaged in my leadership style, ensuring that my team felt supported and empowered to deliver exceptional results. Moving forward, I am committed to applying these lessons to drive both immediate and long-term success in future projects, leveraging my ability to balance urgent needs with strategic goals effectively.
