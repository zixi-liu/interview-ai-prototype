**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me a time when you linked two or more problems together and identified an underlying issue.

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer at a healthcare technology company, we faced significant challenges with our patient data management system, specifically intermittent errors in patient data synchronization and occasional system slowdowns during data retrieval. Recognizing the potential interconnection between these issues, I took ownership of the investigation to identify the underlying cause. 

I independently analyzed system logs and error patterns, writing scripts to correlate the timing of errors and slowdowns, which accounted for about 70% of my investigative work. I hypothesized that the issues were linked to the way data was being handled and stored. To validate my findings, I collaborated with the database team to review the queries I identified and worked closely with the operations team to understand system behavior during peak usage times.

Through this thorough analysis, I discovered that an inefficient database query was causing a lock-up in the system, leading to both the synchronization errors and the slowdowns. After implementing a fix that included a database schema change, I tracked several key metrics over the following two months: the synchronization error rate dropped from 8% to 0.2%, data retrieval time improved from an average of 3.5 seconds to 0.8 seconds, system uptime increased from 94% to 99.5%, and user-reported slowdowns decreased from 15-20 per week to just 1-2. Additionally, the problematic query's performance improved dramatically, going from 4 seconds to just 0.3 seconds.

I communicated my findings and proposed solutions through a detailed report and a presentation during a team meeting, which included visualizations that clearly illustrated the correlation between the issues and the effectiveness of the fix. The team appreciated the depth of my analysis, and management was pleased with the significant improvement in system reliability.

While implementing the fix, I faced challenges due to the required database schema change, which raised concerns among team members about potential risks. I addressed these by developing a comprehensive rollback plan, thoroughly testing the fix in a staging environment, and scheduling the change during a low-traffic period to minimize risk.

This experience reinforced several key lessons: the importance of identifying patterns and correlations when multiple issues arise, the necessity of addressing root causes rather than just symptoms, and the value of using data to guide investigations. I now consistently apply these lessons in my work, ensuring that I document processes and measure improvements to validate the effectiveness of solutions.
