Describe a time you had to explain a complex technical concept to someone non-technical.

## Answer

Situation: At my previous job as the lead developer for a machine learning feature, we were tasked with enhancing our product to support a marketing campaign. During a team meeting, a non-technical stakeholder from the marketing department expressed a need to understand how this feature worked, as it was crucial for their upcoming campaign. Task: My responsibility was to explain the complex machine learning algorithms in a way that was accessible and relevant to their work. Action: I prepared a comprehensive presentation that avoided technical jargon and focused on the fundamentals. Before the presentation, I met with the marketing team lead to understand their goals and concerns, ensuring my explanation aligned with their needs. After the presentation, I followed up with a summary email and scheduled a second meeting to address any further questions. The marketing team was so impressed that they decided to highlight the 'smart' and 'personalized' aspects of the feature in their messaging, which they hadn't planned to do initially. As a result, their campaign performed 25% better than previous ones, and they requested my review of their marketing materials to ensure technical accuracy. Result: The stakeholder left the meeting with a clear understanding of the feature and its business impact, reinforcing the importance of effective communication in technical roles. This experience not only enhanced my communication skills but also deepened my understanding of cross-departmental collaboration and the significance of aligning technical developments with business objectives. It taught me the value of thorough preparation and adaptability in communication, which I continue to apply in my work.

## Answer

Result: The stakeholder appreciated the clear and relatable explanation. They left the meeting with a good understanding of how the feature worked and how it could be leveraged in their marketing strategies. This experience reinforced to me the importance of effective communication skills in technical roles, especially the ability to convey complex concepts in simple terms. It also highlighted the value of cross-departmental collaboration in a tech-driven workplace.
