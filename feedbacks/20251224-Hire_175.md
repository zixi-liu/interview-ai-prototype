**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Tell me about a time when you had to make a decision without all the information you needed.

## Answer

In my previous role as a Senior Software Engineer at an e-commerce company, I took full ownership of a critical initiative to overhaul our online payment system to ensure compliance with new regulatory standards, which had a non-negotiable deadline. When our payment gateway provider unexpectedly delayed the delivery of essential API documentation, I recognized that this could lead to significant project delays, jeopardizing both our compliance status and potential revenue. Understanding the urgency, I made the decisive choice to proceed with developing a mock integration rather than waiting for the documentation, which ultimately saved us about 2 weeks‚Äîcrucial time for meeting our regulatory deadline.

I conducted a thorough analysis of the available information, pinpointing specific gaps in the API documentation and making informed assumptions about the missing components. To validate these assumptions, I proactively engaged with a senior colleague who had extensive experience in similar integrations. I prepared specific questions and scheduled a focused 30-minute session to respect their time, which resulted in invaluable insights that refined our approach. 

I then developed a mock version of the integration in a sandbox environment, achieving a 95% success rate in testing. I tracked several key metrics, including a response time averaging 180ms, an error rate of less than 0.1%, and compatibility, where our mock matched 92% of the actual API when we eventually received the real documentation. This mock integration not only provided a solid foundation for our strategy but also instilled confidence across the team to proceed with implementation.

Recognizing the importance of adhering to industry standards, I reached out to another payment gateway provider for benchmarking purposes, which enriched our integration strategy and ensured we followed best practices for reliability and security. Throughout this process, I maintained transparent communication with our project manager and the entire team, providing regular updates on our progress and potential risks. I facilitated cross-team meetings to ensure alignment and gather feedback, fostering a collaborative environment that was crucial for our success.

When we finally received the complete API documentation from our original provider, I was pleased to find that our hypotheses were largely accurate, requiring only minor adjustments to finalize the integration. This proactive and strategic approach allowed us to meet our compliance deadline, resulting in a remarkable 30% improvement in transaction processing speed and a 40% reduction in payment-related errors, significantly enhancing the overall security and reliability of our payment system.

Reflecting on this experience, I learned the importance of decisive action, leveraging team expertise, and maintaining clear communication. I realized that I could have been more persistent in following up with the gateway provider for the documentation, and in the future, I would enhance my risk assessment framework by documenting my decision-making process, including assumptions and identified risks, and creating a decision log to track key decisions and their rationale. I am confident that these skills and insights will enable me to contribute positively to any team I join, ensuring we not only meet but exceed our goals.
