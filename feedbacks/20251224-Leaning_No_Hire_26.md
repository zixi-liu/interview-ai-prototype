## Question

How do you prioritize your workload? What do you do when your work feels like it's just too much to get done?

## Answer

In my role as a Senior Software Developer, I take full ownership of my workload by employing a prioritization framework that combines the Eisenhower Matrix with Agile methodologies. This allows me to effectively balance urgency and importance while ensuring alignment with broader team goals. For example, during a particularly demanding week, I faced four urgent tasks: a critical bug fix, a feature deadline, a code review, and meeting preparation. By prioritizing the bug fix first, followed by the feature deadline, and then the code review, I was able to quickly resolve the bug, meet the feature deadline, and complete the code review efficiently. This experience reinforced the importance of focusing on what truly matters and demonstrated my ability to influence project outcomes beyond my immediate tasks.

When managing multiple concurrent projects, I proactively organized a cross-functional team meeting to reassess our priorities. I presented a detailed list of tasks with estimated times and collaboratively worked with my manager to reprioritize our efforts. We decided to drop a low-priority project and delay another, allowing me to concentrate on the most critical project. I also delegated some tasks to a teammate, which resulted in delivering high-quality work and significantly reduced my stress levels. This experience taught me that it's essential to communicate when overwhelmed and seek support, ultimately leading to better project outcomes.

To ensure effective delegation, I focus on aligning tasks with the growth of junior developers while meeting project deadlines. I track on-time delivery, aiming for over 90%, and maintain an 85% task completion rate. I also solicit feedback from my manager regarding my workload management, which has been positive.
