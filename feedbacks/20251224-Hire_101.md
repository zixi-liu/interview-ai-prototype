**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Describe a time when you had to give someone difficult feedback. How did you handle it?

## Answer

In my role as a senior software developer at a tech company, I faced a significant challenge when I needed to provide constructive feedback to a junior developer, Alex, who was struggling with documentation‚Äîa critical factor for our team's efficiency and scalability. Understanding that high-quality documentation directly impacts both individual performance and team productivity, I took full ownership of the mentoring process, recognizing its implications for our overall success.

I scheduled a one-on-one meeting with Alex in a supportive environment, where I began by praising his strong coding abilities to build rapport and trust. I framed the documentation issue as an opportunity for growth rather than a failure, sharing my own experiences with similar challenges early in my career. I articulated the importance of documentation in our workflow, linking it to our team's success and the seamless onboarding of new hires. To ensure my feedback was actionable, I provided specific examples of well-documented code from our projects and shared tailored resources to help him enhance his documentation practices.

To facilitate structured improvement, I established clear objectives, aiming for a 60% increase in the clarity and completeness of his documentation within three months. We conducted an initial audit of his work, establishing a baseline documentation coverage of 15%, which allowed us to track progress effectively. I implemented regular review sessions, including pair programming, where we documented code together, empowering Alex to take ownership of his development while fostering a culture of accountability within our team.

The results were impressive: by the end of the follow-up period, Alex achieved a documentation coverage of about 75%. I also tracked the number of questions other developers asked about his code, which decreased from 3-4 questions per week to about 1 per month. Additionally, code reviews for his pull requests were approved faster because reviewers could understand his code more easily. I shared these metrics with Alex in a follow-up conversation, which he found motivating and reinforcing.

Recognizing the broader implications of this experience, I proactively organized a team workshop focused on documentation best practices. I presented data illustrating the positive impact of clear documentation on our workflow, which led to a team-wide commitment to prioritize clarity and completeness. Post-workshop surveys indicated a 40% increase in team members' confidence in their documentation skills, and we observed a 20% improvement in project delivery timelines due to reduced onboarding time for new team members.

Throughout this process, I encountered challenges, such as initial resistance from Alex regarding the feedback. I addressed this by fostering an open dialogue, encouraging him to express his concerns, and collaboratively finding solutions that made the feedback feel more like a partnership in improvement rather than criticism. This approach not only helped Alex embrace the feedback but also reinforced a culture of open communication within our team.

This experience reinforced my commitment to mentorship and highlighted the importance of delivering constructive feedback in a way that promotes growth and accountability. It equipped me with valuable insights that I continue to apply as I mentor other team members, ensuring our collective output remains high-quality and maintainable. Through this initiative, I not only enhanced Alex's skills but also significantly contributed to our team's culture of collaboration and shared responsibility. My ownership of this mentoring initiative, coupled with the measurable improvements across the organization, underscores my ability to influence and drive enhancements that align with our goals for high performance and continuous learning.
