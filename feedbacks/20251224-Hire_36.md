# Red Flag Evaluation (20251224)

**Rating**: Hire

## Question

Describe a time when there was a conflict within your team. How did you help resolve the conflict? Did you do anything to prevent it in the future?

## Answer

In my previous role as a software developer at a mid-sized tech company, we were working on a significant update for our main product when a conflict arose between two team members, John and Sarah, regarding the implementation approach for a crucial feature. John advocated for an innovative, untested method, while Sarah supported a traditional, proven approach. The disagreement escalated, impacting team morale and productivity.

As a senior member of the team, I took ownership of the situation by first meeting with John and Sarah individually to understand their perspectives and acknowledge the merits of their respective views. I made it a point to ensure they felt equally valued in the decision-making process by giving each person equal time to present their case during a team meeting, literally timing their presentations to ensure fairness. When the discussion became heated, I reframed the question from 'which approach is better' to 'what criteria should we use to evaluate both approaches?' This shift transformed the conversation into a collaborative problem-solving session.

I proposed that we prototype both approaches in a controlled environment to objectively assess their viability, which was a key turning point in our discussion. This data-driven approach allowed us to make an informed decision rather than relying solely on opinions. I facilitated the meeting by ensuring that all voices were heard and respected, asking clarifying questions, and managing the dynamics to keep the discussion focused on technical merits rather than personal attacks. When tensions rose, I redirected the conversation back to the technical aspects and suggested a short break to reset the energy, which proved effective in fostering a more productive dialogue.

The prototyping exercise ultimately revealed that while John‚Äôs method had innovative potential, it lacked the stability required for our current project. We decided to proceed with Sarah‚Äôs approach but agreed to explore John's method in a future project. This resolution was accepted by both parties, and I observed a significant improvement in team morale.

To prevent future conflicts, I proposed regular team-building activities and open-forum meetings, which I tracked for effectiveness. Over three months, team satisfaction survey scores improved from 3.2 to 4.5 out of 5, and the number of conflicts reported in our retrospectives decreased from 2-3 per month to about 1 every two months. Participation in the open-forum meetings was consistently high, with 85-90% attendance. I also noted an increase in the quality of code review feedback, which became more constructive and less defensive. These metrics demonstrated the positive impact of my initiatives, and I presented them to our manager, who was impressed with the improvements.

This experience taught me the importance of proactive conflict resolution and effective communication in maintaining a collaborative team environment. I recognized that I could have intervened earlier and set clearer ground rules for discussions, and I plan to incorporate these lessons into my future approaches to conflict resolution.
